# TELOS Framework

## Overview

TELOS (Greek word for "purpose" or "end goal") is a structured self-interview framework that helps transform abstract concepts about identity and goals into concrete, actionable context that AI systems can understand and utilize.

**Why it matters:**
- Provides a "North Star" for AI interactions
- Gives AI answers directionality and personalization
- Enables AI to provide contextually relevant responses aligned with your goals
- Creates a living document that evolves with you

## The Six Layers

TELOS consists of six hierarchical layers, from problem identification to daily action:

### 1. Problems (What bothers you?)

Not "what's your goal," but "what's bothering you?"

Most human motivation comes from dissatisfaction with current states.

**Guidelines:**
- Be specific and concrete
- Can be personal problems or world problems
- Examples:
  - "My parents worked three jobs each and had no time for me, which caused..."
  - "Current vulnerability management tools don't understand how engineering teams actually work"
  - "Most people feel their ideas aren't worth sharing"

**Daniel's Example:**
- Personal: Lack of parental time during childhood
- Professional: Inadequate tools for engineering teams
- Societal: People undervaluing their own insights

### 2. Goals (What do you want to change?)

Not "what do you want," but "what do you want to change?"

This is the transformation from problem to direction.

**Core Goal Formulation:**
- Identify the desired state change
- Connect goals back to identified problems
- Make goals actionable but not task-specific

**Daniel's Core Goal:** "Human Activation" - activating more people to realize they have ideas worth sharing

**Manifested Across Projects:**
- PAI project: Enable individuals to build their own AI infrastructure
- Fabric project: Open source AI tools
- Unsupervised Learning: Share ideas and insights publicly

### 3. Challenges (What's stopping you?)

Often skipped, but critical. Without explicit barriers, AI can't help you navigate around them.

**Types of Obstacles:**
1. **Technical**: "I don't know how to code"
2. **Resource**: "I don't have time"
3. **Psychological**: "I don't feel qualified enough"
4. **Systemic**: "Existing tools are too complex"

**Daniel's Example:**
- Goal: Get more people using personal AI systems
- Barrier: "Most people aren't technical; command-line is too hard"
- Solution Implication: PAI must be accessible to non-technical users

### 4. Strategies (How will you do it?)

The bridge from "want" to "action." Methodology-level approaches, not task lists.

**Daniel's Strategies:**
1. Start with open source projects to lower barriers
2. Use Markdown files instead of databases for portability
3. Solve your own problems first, then scale to others
4. Continuously share the process to activate others

### 5. Projects (What are you working on now?)

Current active initiatives, each connecting back to goals and strategies.

**Daniel's Projects:**
1. **PAI**: Personal AI infrastructure framework
2. **Fabric**: AI tool collection
3. **Unsupervised Learning**: Weekly newsletter
4. Client consulting work
5. Vulnerability bounty research

Each project should trace back to:
- Which problems it addresses
- Which goals it advances
- Which strategies it implements

### 6. Workflows (What do you actually do every day?)

Critical for AI to assist you effectively. AI needs to understand your actual daily routines.

**Daniel's Workflow:**
1. Morning walks: Use Limitless to capture ideas via voice recording
2. Return: Let AI organize these ideas
3. Process: Use "parliamentary debate" to have multiple AI personas challenge the idea
4. Real-time editing: Shape ideas into articles
5. One-click publishing: Post to X and LinkedIn

**Flow:** From "idea" to "social media publication," all AI-supported.

## Why TELOS Works

### 1. Provides AI with a "North Star"

Every AI interaction happens with context:
- Which goal does this question relate to?
- In which project's context are you asking?
- What are your barriers (to avoid these pitfalls)?

**Daniel's Friend Example (Cardiologist + Bug Bounty Hunter):**

After loading his TELOS into the system, AI understands:
- His goal: Find more vulnerabilities, earn more bounties
- His expertise: Client-side vulnerabilities
- His workflow: Test during clinic gaps
- His techniques: Unique testing methods

Result: Simply giving AI a target company triggers:
- Reconnaissance using his methodology
- Focus on client-side vulnerabilities
- Generate test scripts in his style
- Organize results his way

**Outcome:** Dramatic increase in vulnerability discoveries.

### 2. Gives AI Responses Directionality

Daniel's "Universal Algorithm" concept:

**From current state to ideal state**

Every AI assistance essentially executes this algorithm:
1. Current state: Where you are now
2. Ideal state: Where you want to go
3. Process: How to get there

TELOS defines the "ideal state."

- **Without TELOS:** AI gives generic answers
- **With TELOS:** AI knows what's optimal **for you**

### 3. It Evolves

This is the most powerful aspect.

Daniel's system has an "upgrade skills" feature:

When he:
1. Watches a YouTube video
2. Reads a book
3. Learns new technology

He can have AI:
1. Read the new knowledge
2. Compare against his TELOS
3. Suggest system upgrades

**Example:**
After reading a rhetoric book, AI suggests:
> "Based on this book's principles, I recommend:
> 1. Add these rhetorical techniques to your writing skills
> 2. Adjust your blog workflow to include rhetoric checks
> 3. Update your style guide"

AI helps AI become more aligned with you.

## Implementation Process

### Step 1: Dump

Find quiet time, answer these questions via voice or text:

1. What bothers you? (personal, professional, societal)
2. If you had a magic wand, what would you change?
3. What are you working on now? Why?
4. What's preventing you from doing better?

### Step 2: Structure

Organize content using the TELOS framework:

```markdown
# TELOS

## Problems

- I feel most people...
- Existing tools...
- I always...

## Goals

- I want to help...
- I want to create...
- I want to learn...

## Challenges

- Technical: ...
- Time: ...
- Psychological: ...

## Strategies

- Through open source...
- Start with small projects...
- Continuous sharing...

## Projects

- Project A: ...
- Project B: ...

## Workflows

- Morning: ...
- Writing process: ...
- Research process: ...
```

### Step 3: Load into System

This is the PAI core. Every time you start Claude Code (or other AI systems), it reads this TELOS file.

**Daniel's setup:**
- ~10,000 tokens
- Loaded on every session start

### Step 4: Iterate

After using it for a while, you'll discover:
1. Some goals changed
2. Some barriers disappeared
3. New problems emerged

**Update cycle:** Review and update TELOS at least monthly.

## Daniel's Key Insight

**TELOS's most important function isn't making AI smarter—it's making you clearer.**

The process of defining your TELOS forces clarity about your own direction, which then enables AI to serve you effectively.

## Usage Instructions for AI

When this skill is invoked, the AI should:

1. **Read and parse** the user's TELOS document
2. **Align responses** with their stated goals and strategies
3. **Consider barriers** when suggesting solutions
4. **Contextualize advice** within their active projects
5. **Reference workflows** when offering process improvements

**Response patterns:**
- Connect suggestions back to stated goals
- Avoid solutions that conflict with identified barriers
- Prioritize actions advancing current projects
- Match advice to documented workflows
- Suggest TELOS updates when goals appear to shift

## Best Practices

- **Be specific** in all sections (vague TELOS = vague AI assistance)
- **Connect layers** (each project should trace back to goals and strategies)
- **Update regularly** (TELOS is a living document, not static)
- **Use examples** (concrete scenarios help AI understand better)
- **Document trade-offs** (why you chose certain strategies over others)
- **Include constraints** (time, resources, technical limitations)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Don't skip the "Problems" section and jump straight to goals
- Don't make goals so abstract they can't guide action
- Don't list projects without connecting them to your goals
- Don't forget daily workflows (this is where AI helps most)
- Don't treat TELOS as static—it should evolve with you
